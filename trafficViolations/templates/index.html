The Story of Traffic Violations

We wanted to look into the traffic violations for a specific area over time, to see if traffic stops are getting less frequent or more frequent.
Traffic violations include citations and warnings for cases such as not wearing seatbelt, property damage, personal damage, speeding etc.,.
A four year (2012 to 2016) worth of traffic violations data for Montgomery County, MD were found in a CSV from Kaggle called “Traffic Violations in USA”. However, the CSV contained to much data to represent and needed cleaning up.
Our hypothesis was that the number of violations would be going up, but year over year data shows an improving trend of violations going down.


What does data say?

A simple mean analysis, as illustrated by box plot to the right, shows that hypothesis is indeed supported with the traffic violations have risen since 2012 and reached a peak of 13,000 violaitions per qtr in 2015 before starting a downward trend in 2016.
The next question is, will the downward trend continue? To answer this we plotted Year on Year change analysis (plot above). From the plot we see

  • From 2012 to mid 2014, Year on Year change rose significantly pushing the violation count beyond 40K
  • However from last Qtr of 2014, the Year on Year change is on negative trend such that violations dropped significantly in 2016

 Depicting district wise contribution to YOY change of traffic violation

So, which district contributed to rise and fall of trends?
A contribution % chart (on left) was plotted, to understand the contribution increasing and decreasing trend.
As we see, two district, namely 3rd district-Silver spring and 4th district-Wheaton had a significant role in these trends. The contribution from these two district has mainly determined the overall trend of violations
